Research Interests

My research experience includes a project at the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams, where I applied the Reduced Basis Method to streamline high-dimensional nuclear many-body interactions into two-level systems. Collaborating with the Theory Alliance at FRIB, I contributed to a chapter in Quantum Computing Applications in Nuclear Physics, focusing on Bloch sphere representations and quantum gate assembly for Qiskit-based simulations.

At the Center of Applied Quantum Technology in Stuttgart, I designed VHDL/Verilog PID controllers on Xilinx FPGAs for optical levitation systems. At ATF Cooling GmbH, I engineered Red Pitaya FPGA-based PID controllers with high-speed ADC/DAC interfaces to stabilize industrial chillers using adaptive control algorithms.

In experimental nanofabrication, I fabricated Josephson junctions in cleanrooms—mastering photolithography, e-beam lithography, etching, sputtering, and ion milling—and performed DC Josephson measurements with SQUIDs to study Fraunhofer-pattern phase shifts and critical current suppression mechanisms, including spin-triplet pairing and ferromagnetic exchange.

My computational physics work includes training message-passing neural networks at the Institute for Computer Physics in Stuttgart to predict material properties, demonstrating how GNNs can rival traditional quantum simulations in efficiency and accuracy.
